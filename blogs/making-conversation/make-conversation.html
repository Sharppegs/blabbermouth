Top Tips for a Successful Conversation

March 1st, 2023

One of the most important stages of learning a language is knowing how to express yourself.

During a conversation, or in FCE, CAE or IELTS exams, we need to agree, disagree and give our personal viewpoint.

Let’s look at some useful expressions we can use!

How to Agree

So, someone has expressed an idea that you like and you want to show them you agree. You can use:

“I totally agree”

“Absolutely!”

“I couldn’t agree more”

“I know what you mean"

“Exactly!”

“We’re on the same page”

“I'm with you'”

“That’s how I see it, too"

How to Disagree

When disagreeing in English, many of the phrases are polite and respectful. You can use these expressions to tell someone that you feel the opposite way, while still respecting their view.

“I hear you, but…”

“I see where you’re coming from, however…”

“That’s a good point, but…"

“I see what you’re saying, but…"

Sometimes we want someone to know that we strongly disagree with something that they have said, in which case, we can say…

“I don’t agree with that at all”

“I wouldn’t say that”

“You've got to be kidding!" (very strong!)

Personalise the Topic

Whether you agree or disagree, it’s important to support your ideas with some evidence. This helps the other person understand your point of view.

Usually, we use personal experiences to give examples.

“In my experience…”

“I remember once when…”

“This topic is personal for me because…"

“I actually know something about this because…"

So, next time you find yourself with a group of friends, try out some of these useful phrases!
